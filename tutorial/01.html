Interactive Tutorial

Learn how to annotate pages in accordance with the annotation guidelines.

Intro

This tutorial will help you getting used to the annotation guidelines. It assumes you are familiar with the manual (click to open a new window, suitable for side-by-side viewing with this one. Or print the pdf).
The tutorial walks you through 11 particular problems; successful completion will take about 15 minutes.

How it works

The tutorial pretty much works like annotation on a real corpus: you
  • annotate a Web page,
  • submit it, and
  • get the next one.
However, we inserted a validation step after submission: The current page will be re-displayed and will show how your annotations diverged from ours:
  • Everything you got right will be light-colored, i.e. your annotation in light color.
  • Everything you got wrong will be highlighted as it was supposed to be.
Please take your time to go through this feedback carefully - you won't get any when working on the real corpus.

After inspection, you can click the Next Page button in the notification box at the top, or just use Grab Page from Tutorial from the KrdWrd status-bar menu, to advance. You can return to your last tutorial step at any time by hitting Start Tutorial once again. Reset it by deleting your annotations on your My Stats page.

Start the tutorial

Now let's get going. Move around your mouse on this page. You did that right from the start, didn't you? Busy little bees you are! You should get fancy highlighting wherever you go.
  • Annotate some of the text here to get used to the keyboard shortcuts.
  • Then click on Submit in the KrdWrd menu.
    • You will get back to this page, this time without validation result.
  • Hit Next Page to get going with the 11 real pages.
