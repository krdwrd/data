I've found Dennis King's pronunciations to be as good as any. There are, of course, three major dialects: Ulster, Connact and Munster. These seem to have variations in pronouncing things like "d" before a short vowel and "bh" as "v" or as a "w."

I'd be interested in knowing where this information about the Tuatha having crystal swan ships is to be found. Quite often information like this comes more from spiritual visions rather than physical evidence. Both have impacts on the mind and our outlooks on life.

The stories I've read and heard about the Tuatha Dé Danann has them going underground and into the hollow hills after their defeat at the hands of the Sons of Mil. This is typically the results for the older gods of a culture when newer, more dominant  people arrive in an area. They are said to sink into the ground or the sea.This is a metaphor perhaps for them becoming submerged in the minds and the subconscious as daily life embraces newer concepts and other gods. That way they can still live within a person and not be without a people.

The Sidhe are the Tuatha Dé and live within the Land, Sky and Sea. They are a part of the spirit and magic within each. There are other more elemental spirits in these realms as well. To me, the Fomorii are the more untamed, chaotic and wilder spirits that empower the principles of being, creation and destruction, while the Tuatha Dé are those who provide a way to to influence these things through law, skill and order. The islands "to the West" are many and varied in Irish mythology. Some are havens and homes to the Sidhe while others center around principles and other types of entities/beings.
