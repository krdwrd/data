<h2><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">
Subepithelial Microvasculature in Large Airways Observed by High-Magnification Bronchovideoscope</kw><sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">*</kw></sup>
</h2>

<strong>
<nobr><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Gen Yamada, MD</kw></nobr><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">; 
</kw><nobr><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Hiroki Takahashi, MD</kw></nobr><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">;

&nbsp;<br>


<i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">Background:</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> The bronchial vasculature serves important functions</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">and is modified in a variety of pulmonary and airway diseases.</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">The remarkable ability of the bronchial vasculature to undergo</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">remodeling has implications for disease pathogenesis. However,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">there is very little information on normal bronchial circulation.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

<i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">Objectives:</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> The aim of this study was to obtain information</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">on bronchial microvessels in large airways using a high-magnification</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchovideoscope.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

<i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">Methods and patients:</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> Recently, we developed a high-magnification</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchovideoscope (XBF-200HM3 [side-viewing type]) in cooperation</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">with Olympus Medical Systems. This bronchovideoscope can provide</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">information on the bronchial mucosa with a maximum magnification</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of 110 times. Between August 2000 and July 2004, 26 patients</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">without abnormalities in the large airways were enrolled into</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">this study. Patients underwent conventional bronchoscopy and</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">subsequent bronchoscopy with the high-magnification bronchovideoscope.</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">After the bronchoscopic examination, we calculated the vessel</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">area ratios and hemoglobin indexes of images made with the high-magnification</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchovideoscope by using appropriate software. In addition,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">we compared the findings obtained with the high-magnification</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchovideoscope of the 26 subjects with microscopic findings</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of autopsied tracheas of two patients without abnormalities.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

<i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">Results:</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> Many ramifying subepithelial microvessels of large</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">airways were mainly observed in intercartilage and membranous</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portions, whereas only a few microvessels were seen in cartilage</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portions. Histologically, these subepithelial microvessels were</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">thought to be distributed within approximately 800 and 500 µm</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">beneath the surface of the intercartilage portions and membranous</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portions, respectively. Vessel area ratios of the intercartilage</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portions were significantly higher than those of the cartilage</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">and membranous portions. The hemoglobin indexes of the intercartilage</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portions were significantly higher than those of the cartilage</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">and membranous portions, and these indexes were also significantly</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">higher in the membranous portion than in the cartilage portion.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

<i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">Conclusions:</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> A dense concentration of subepithelial microvessels</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">was mainly observed in the intercartilage portion, indicating</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">an increase in submucosal circulation. This high-magnification</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchovideoscope is a useful tool for observing and evaluating</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the subepithelial microvessels in large airways.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>



</p><p><strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Key Words:</kw></strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1"> bronchial circulation • extrapulmonary bronchus • subepithelial vasculature • high-magnification bronchovideoscope</kw></p><p>

&nbsp;<br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

The bronchial vasculature serves important functions and is</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">modified by a variety of pulmonary and airway diseases. Congestion</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of the bronchial vasculature may narrow the airway lumen in</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">inflammatory airway diseases, and the formation of new bronchial</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">vessels (</kw><i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">ie</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">, angiogenesis) is implicated in the pathology of</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">a variety of chronic inflammatory, infectious, or ischemic pulmonary</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">diseases. The remarkable ability of the bronchial vasculature</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">to undergo remodeling has implications for disease pathogenesis.</kw><sup><a href="#B1"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">1</kw></a></sup><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">The bronchial microvasculature provides nutrient blood flow</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">to the airway epithelium and blood flow changes in neural and</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">humoral stimuli, and plays a role in the conditioning of inspired</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">air.</kw><sup><a href="#B1"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">1</kw></a></sup><sup><a href="#B2"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">2</kw></a></sup><sup><a href="#B3"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">3</kw></a></sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> However, observation of the bronchial microvasculature</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">by a conventional bronchoscope provides us with little information.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

Recently, we developed a prototype of a high-magnification bronchovideoscope</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">(XBF-200HM3) in cooperation with Olympus Medical Systems (Tokyo,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">Japan). This side-viewing bronchovideoscope provides information</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">on the bronchial mucosa with a maximum magnification of 110</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">times on a 14-inch video monitor, showing the subepithelial</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchial microvasculature in large airways.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

In this study, we used the high-magnification bronchovideoscope</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">to observe the subepithelial microvasculature of tracheas and</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">extrapulmonary bronchi with no pathologic findings on conventional</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchoscopy. Next, we evaluated the subepithelial vascular</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">circulation in each high-magnification image by calculating</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the vessel area ratio and hemoglobin index. The hemoglobin index</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">is obtained by electron endoscopic measurement of hemoglobin</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">volume.</kw><sup><a href="#B4"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">4</kw></a></sup><sup><a href="#B5"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">5</kw></a></sup><sup><a href="#B6"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">6</kw></a></sup><sup> </sup></p><p>

<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">Patients</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

Using the high-magnification bronchovideoscope, we examined</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the bronchial vasculature in 26 patients (age range, 34 to 83</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">years; 18 men and 8 women) without intrabronchial abnormalities.</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">We excluded the patients who had abnormal findings in the trachea</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">and extrapulmonary bronchi using a conventional bronchoscope.</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">In order to eliminate the influence of other diseases on the</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchial mucosa, we also excluded patients who had complications,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">including allergic diseases, granulomatous diseases (</kw><i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">eg</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">, sarcoidosis),</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">collagen diseases, cardiovascular diseases, and infectious diseases.</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">All patients were informed preoperatively and gave their consent.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

In addition, the distribution of subepithelial microvessels</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">in the tracheas of two male patients who had been autopsied</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">(ages, 62 and 66) and who had both died of gastric cancer, was</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">examined. Using the high-magnification bronchovideoscope, we</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">compared the longitudinal distribution of microvessels of the</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">formalin-fixed specimens of the tracheas obtained at autopsy.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">High-Magnification Bronchovideoscope</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

The high-magnification bronchovideoscope is 6.3 mm in diameter.</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">This flexible bronchovideoscope is equipped with a charge-coupled</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">device (CCD) at its distal end (</kw><a href="#F1"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">Fig 1</kw></a><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">
). By using the prism,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the images of the bronchial mucosa are captured by the CCD through</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">a magnifying objective lens. As with a conventional bronchovideoscope,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">optical information is converted to electric signals by the</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">CCD and transmitted to the video processor (EVIS CV-240; Olympus</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">Medical Systems). The signals from the high-magnification bronchovideoscope</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">were reconstructed into visual signals by the video processor</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">and projected onto a 14-inch video monitor.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

The observation depth was 1 to 3 mm, and the images of the bronchial</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">mucosa were visualized with a magnification power of 55 to 110</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">times on a video monitor according to the distance between the</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">objective lens and the bronchial surface. The view angle was</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">40°, but little distortion is seen. In addition, a forward-viewing</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">optical fiberscope was attached to act as a guide. By using</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">this front-viewing system, we confirmed the orientation and</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">approached the endobronchial destinations while obtaining a</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">high-magnification view.</kw><sup> </sup><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

We observed the trachea and extrapulmonary bronchi of these</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">patients under local anesthesia. In brief, after muscle injection</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of atropin sulfate, 0.5 mg, and pentazocin, 15 mg, and the nebulization</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of 12 mL of a 2% lidocaine solution, a conventional bronchovideoscope</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">(BF-P200, BF-1T200, or BF-T200; Olympus Medical Systems) was</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">orally inserted into the trachea under local anesthesia, and</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the endobronchial lumen was examined. After conventional bronchoscopy</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">was performed, we inserted the high-magnification bronchovideoscope</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">into the trachea by using the forward-viewing system. We observed</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the trachea and extrapulmonary bronchi enfacely under high magnification.</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">Each bronchoscopic image obtained using the high-magnification</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchovideoscope was recorded in an electronic file.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">Calculation of Vessel Area Ratio and Hemoglobin Index</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

Forty-nine images of the cartilage portion, 52 images of the</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">intercartilage portion, and 44 images of membranous portion</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">from the 26 healthy subjects were analyzed. In order to evaluate</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the microvessel distribution obtained by the high-magnification</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchovideoscope, the vessel area ratio and hemoglobin index</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">were calculated using appropriate software (SolemioENDO ProStudy;</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">Olympus Medical Systems).</kw><sup> </sup></p><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

In brief, these parameters were calculated and determined as</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">follows. First, a region of interest (ROI) was selected from</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the original high-magnification bronchovideoscope images on</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">a computer display, and vascular images in the ROI were obtained.</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">The microvessels were depicted as pixels. The vessel area ratio</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">was determined as the number of pixels in the vessel area within</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the ROI. Next, the hemoglobin index was calculated.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-2">Statistical Analysis</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

Results were expressed as the mean ± SD. Differences</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">in vessel area ratios and hemoglobin indexes were compared using</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the Mann-Whitney </kw><i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">U</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> test.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

The correlation between vessel area ratios and hemoglobin indexes</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">was analyzed using the Spearman signed rank test. Differences</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">were considered to be statistically significant when the p value</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">was &lt; 0.05.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>

<a href="#SEC2"><img alt=" " src="/icons/toc/uarrow.gif" border="0" width="11" height="9" hspace="5"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Results</kw><br></a>

<a href="#BIBL"><img alt=" " src="/icons/toc/darrow.gif" border="0" width="11" height="9" hspace="5"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">References</kw><br></a>
</font></th></tr></tbody></table>
&nbsp;<br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

The subepithelial structure of the normal bronchial tree consists</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">mainly of cartilage rings, longitudinal and circular muscular</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">folds, and subepithelial vessels. These vessels were observed</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">through the transparent epithelium, which was covered with a</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">mucosal layer, and were thought to form a venous network in</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">the subepithelial layer of the bronchus.</kw><sup><a href="#B2"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">2</kw></a></sup><sup><a href="#B3"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">3</kw></a></sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> Many ramifying microvessels</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">were clearly observed by the high-magnification bronchovideoscope</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">because both the side view and high magnification were achieved.</kw><sup> </sup><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

The large airway, including the trachea and extrapulmonary bronchi,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">is anatomically divided into the following three parts: cartilage</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portion; intercartilage portion; and membranous portion. As</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">seen with a conventional bronchovideoscope, the distribution</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of the subepithelial venous network seems to be random. However,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">according to our observations with the high-magnification bronchovideoscope,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">subepithelial microvessels were concentrated mainly in the intercartilage</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portions, whereas there were few microvessels in the cartilage</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portions. Some microvessels were also observed in the membranous</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">portions in addition to the longitudinal folds. Similarly, the</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">intercartilage portions had the highest hemoglobin index values</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">among the three areas. The value of the hemoglobin index correlated</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">with the vessel area ratios. These findings indicate an increase</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of microcirculation in the intercartilage portion in the large</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">airways.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

The depth of the visible subepithelial microvessels below the</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">epithelial surface is unclear. A longitudinally sectioned trachea</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">showed that thick vessels were distributed more deeply than</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">thin vessels. According to the findings in the 26 subjects and</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">in the trachea specimens obtained by autopsy in 2 subjects that</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">were obtained with the high-magnification bronchovideoscope,</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">it was speculated that the vessels made visible with this bronchovideoscope</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">were probably distributed within 800 µm below the epithelial</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">surface. Whether or not microvessels are visible depends on</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">their diameter and histologic condition.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">

The bronchial vessels are able to regenerate and form new vessels</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">very rapidly in various conditions.</kw><sup><a href="#B1"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">1</kw></a></sup><sup><a href="#B2"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">2</kw></a></sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> Mucosal blood flow is</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">thought to be influenced by vascular and airway pressures, inspired</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">air conditions, and anatomic neurotransmitters.</kw><sup><a href="#B7"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">7</kw></a></sup><sup><a href="#B8"><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">8</kw></a></sup> <i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">In situ</kw></i><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3"> observation</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of these bronchial changes with high-magnification devices may</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">provide us with new information concerning various bronchial</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">mucosal changes in patients with respiratory diseases such as</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">bronchial asthma, chronic bronchitis, sarcoidosis, and lung</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">cancer. Quantitative analysis of the vascular densities or microcirculation</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">in various bronchial diseases may lead to a better understanding</kw><sup> </sup><kw class="krdwrd-tag-3">of the hemodynamics of these pathologic states.</kw><sup> </sup></p><p>
