	 <div id="itinerary" style="display: none;">
	     
		<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Thursday, October 25, 2007</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">USA</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
Depart from the United States for Thessaloniki, Greece.</kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Friday, October 26</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">THESSALONIKI, Greece</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
Arrive in Thessaloniki (the biblical Thessalonica), named after the sister of Alexander the Great, and transfer to the Electra Palace Hotel, our accommodations for the next two nights. Enjoy a welcome cocktail reception and dinner this evening at the hotel. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Saturday, October 27</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">THESSALONIKI | PELLA | VERGINA | THESSALONIKI</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
In the morning, take an excursion to Pella, capital of Macedonia in the 4th century B.C.&nbsp; and Alexander the Greats birthplace. Tour the site, including the Agora, the Sanctuary of Aphrodite and Cybele, and the museum, exhibiting finds from the site, including several pebble mosaic floors. Then drive to Vergina, where the excavated palace and tombs of Philip II (Alexanders father) are located. Visit the excavations and see the Macedonian burial mounds which dot the plainone of which was confirmed, in 1977, as the tomb of Philip. Also tour the Archaeological Museum, where many of the treasures recovered from the tomb are on display. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Sunday, October 28</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">THESSALONIKI | EMBARK</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
Enjoy time at leisure in the morning followed by an afternoon tour of several of the citys ancient churches, including the 5th-century Church of Hagia Sophia and St. Dimitrios, Greeces largest church, which preserves the tomb of the martyred patron saint of Thessaloniki. Also visit to the Archaeological Museum, which houses a spectacular collection of Macedonia gold and sculpture. Later, transfer to the harbor to embark on </kw><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Corinthian II</kw></em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1"> and set sail. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Monday, October 29</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">CANAKKALE | TROY | CANAKKALE, Turkey</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
Arrive in Canakkale, a port town guarding the entrance to the Dardanellesthe narrow waterway that separates Europe from Asiafor an excursion to the famed archaeological site of Troy. In the spring of 334 B.C. Alexander crossed the Dardanelles (the ancient Hellespont) with an army of 35,000 in tow. They stopped at Troy to offer sacrifices at the Temple of Athena and at the place where Achilles was buried. They later soundly defeated the Persians at the nearby Battle of Granicus, which solidified Alexanders hold over Asia Minor. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Tuesday, October 30</kw><br>
</em><strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">IZMIR, Turkey | EPHESUS or SARDIS</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
From Izmir, visit the great ancient city of Ephesus, one of the most important sites in the history of the ancient world. First renowned in the second millennium B.C. as the center of worship for the great mother-goddess, Cybele (later known as Artemis or Diana), Ephesus contains extensive ruins from the Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine periods. Visit the large theater in which&nbsp;the Ephesians rioted against St. Paul; the Library of Celsus, with its imposing faade; and the Temple of Serapis. Alternatively, visit Sardis, settled as early as 1200 B.C. and held at various times by the Persians, the Macedonians, and the Romans. Visit the ancient synagogue, bath complex, and Temple of Artemis. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Wednesday, October 31</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">DATCHA | KNIDOS</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
From the harbor at Datcha, we call at the seldom-visited ancient site of Knidos, founded by Dorians just after the Trojan War and famed as a center of art and learning.&nbsp; Remains include a Roman tomb with a fine black and white mosaic, a lighthouse, monumental building and the Temple of Aphrodite, which, according to tradition, was renowned in antiquity for its statue of Aphrodite by the famed sculptor Praxiteles. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Thursday, November 1</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">ANTALYA | PERGE | ASPENDOS | ANTALYA</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
Arrive in Antalya, for an excursion to Perge, a Greek city that welcomed Alexander in 333 B.C. According to tradition, Greek settlers founded Perge following the Trojan War, and the city gained importance during the Roman Empire. The citys Hellenistic ruins include its great stadium; the colonnaded and marble main street, which still bears the ruts of wagon wheels; and the theater, decorated with sculptures of scenes from the life of Dionysus, the god of wine and revelry. Continue to Aspendos, which Alexander occupied also in 333 B.C., the highlight of which is the grand 20,000-seat theater commissioned by Marcus Aurelius in the 2nd century A.D.&nbsp; </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Friday, November 2</kw><br>
</em><strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">ALANYA | SIDE | ALANYA</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
From the resort town of Alanya, former stronghold of the Seljuks in the 12th century, travel to one of Turkeys most famous ancient sitesSide. Situated on a narrow peninsula, Side was a leading Eastern Mediterranean port until the 7th century when it came under the control of invading Arabs. See the ancient citys magnificent theater, built on colonnaded arches; the Temple of Apollo; and the extensive Roman baths, which house one of Turkeys finest archaeological collections. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Saturday, November 3</kw><br>
</em><strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">ISKENDERUN | ANTIOCH | ISKENDERUN</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
After a morning at sea, </kw><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Corinthian II</kw></em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1"> calls this morning at Iskenderun (formerly Alexandretta), founded by Alexander to celebrate his victory over Darius III at Issus in 333 B.C.&nbsp; Travel to Antioch, one of the great cities of antiquity founded Seleucus I around 301 B.C. and the capital of the Seleucid dynasty of Syria. The Romans later defeated the Syrians and many of the ruins still in existence date from the time of Roman rule. Tour the site and visit the Archaeological Museum, renowned for its magnificent collection of Roman mosaics. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Sunday, November 4</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">AT SEA</kw><br>
</strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Spend the day at sea cruising toward Egypt. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Monday, November 5</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">PORT SAID | MEMPHIS &amp; SAQQARA OR GIZA &amp; CAIRO | SIWA OASIS, Egypt</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
This morning, </kw><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Corinthian II</kw></em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1"> pulls into Port Said, located at the entrance to the Suez Canal. From here, there will be a choice of two full-day excursions. Travel to the ancient capital of Memphis, where Alexander was declared a Pharaoh, and visit Saqqara, a vast burial ground dominated by the Step Pyramid of Zoser. Alternatively, visit Giza, site of the Pyramids of Cheops and Chephren, built between 2600 and 2530 B.C., and the mysterious Sphinx. Then drive to Cairo to explore Egyptian Museum, a treasure-trove of artifacts chronicling Egypts past.&nbsp; In the late afternoon, rejoin your fellow travelers for a chartered group flight Cairo to Siwa. Overnight at the </kw><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Siwa Paradise Hotel.</kw></em></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Tuesday, November 6</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">SIWA OASIS | ALEXANDRIA</kw></strong> <br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
Awaken today in the famed Siwa Oasis, site of the oracular Temple of Amun. In 331 B.C., Alexander made a pilgrimage to the temple, whose oracle confirmed his divinity as the son of Amun, an ancient Egyptian god whom the Greeks identified with Zeus. Enjoy a full day to explore the Oasis, including a visit to the Temple of Amun, located in the old town of Aghurmi, as well as other ancient ruins. Some archaeologists believe that a large tomb recently discovered in the area may be that of Alexander. In the evening, fly to Alexandria, and reboard </kw><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Corinthian II</kw></em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">.&nbsp; Spend the night docked in port. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Wednesday, November 7</kw></em> <br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">ALEXANDRIA</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
Founded by Alexander in 332 B.C., Alexandria was the capital of Egypt for almost 1,000 years. Little evidence remains of the citys illustrious past as the center of the Hellenistic world. The Pharos lighthouse, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, and the original Bibliotheca Alexandrina, have succumbed to the ravages of time and nature. Recently, the city opened a new Bibliotheca Alexandrina to rival the old. Visit the Antiquities Museum housed there and then continue to Alexandrias ancient ruins. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Thursday, November 8</kw></em> <br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">AT SEA</kw><br>
</strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Spend the day at sea enjoying lectures and the amenities of </kw><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Corinthian II</kw></em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1"> as it cruises across the Mediterranean toward Greece. </kw></p>
<p><em><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">Friday, November 9</kw></em><br>
<strong><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">PIRAEUS | ATHENS, Greece | DISEMBARK | USA</kw></strong><br><kw class="krdwrd-tag-1">
Disembark in Piraeus and transfer to the airport for return flights to the United States. </kw></p>
