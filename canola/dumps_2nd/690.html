China Merchants Buys Shenzhen Port Stakes to Expand (Update3)

By Matthew R. Miller and Neil Denslow

Nov. 16 (Bloomberg) -- China Merchants Holdings (International) Co., a Hong Kong-based port operator, will buy assets in China's second-biggest container port to benefit from rising trade. Its shares rose.

The company will acquire stakes in two port ventures in Shenzhen, southern China, from its parent, it said in a statement late yesterday. China Merchants will pay HK$1.63 billion ($210 million) for the assets, HK$445 million in cash and 51 million new shares.

Chairman Fu Yuning has expanded China Merchants' ports business, including buying a stake in Shanghai in June 2005, as the world's fastest-growing major economy exports more clothing, computers and other goods. China's coastal and river ports handled 21 percent more cargo in the first half of the year.

``The acquisition will help China Merchants further strengthen its strong presence in West Shenzhen and secure the scarce resources, land and shoreline that we think are crucial to China Merchants' long-term sustainable growth,'' Ingrid Wei, a Shanghai-based analyst for UBS Securities Asia Ltd., wrote in a note dated yesterday.

China Merchants shares gained 5.3 percent to HK$26.00 in Hong Kong, after rising as much as 12 percent. The stock, a Hang Seng Index member, has gained 54 percent this year, compared with a 29 percent rise in the benchmark index.

The company will acquire the 40 percent it doesn't already own in ventures at Shenzhen's Mawan Port, including Shenzhen Mawan Wharf Co., Shenzhen Mawan Port Services Co. and Shenzhen Mawan Terminals Co., it said. The ventures control four berths at the port.

Haixing Harbor

China Merchants will also gain control of Haixing Harbor Development Co., which controls four berths at Haixing Harbor at Mawan Port, by raising its stake to 67 percent from 33 percent. China Merchants will also acquire 94,303 square meters of land located at Nanshan in Shenzhen and a port highway, it said.

China Merchants' first-half net income rose 3.8 percent to HK$1.21 billion ($155 million). Sales, excluding contributions from Shanghai and other ports where it has minority stakes, surged 40 percent to HK$1.86 billion.

The new shares represent around 2.15 percent of the company's enlarged share capital, China Merchants said. The issue will raise its parent's stake to 56 percent.

China Merchants, which invests in Hong Kong and six mainland Chinese ports, completed its purchase of a stake in the operator of Shanghai port, China's busiest harbor, last year.

China Merchants, the listed unit of state-owned China Merchants Group, also owns a stake in China International Marine Containers Co., the world's largest maker of sea cargo boxes.

China's coastal and river ports handled a total of 2.2 billion tons in the six months to June, the Ministry of Communications said in July. The number of 20-foot containers handled rose 23 percent from a year to 42.2 million boxes.
