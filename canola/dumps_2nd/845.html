Cruise ships at Roatan

Hi,

I am thinking about making a trip to Roatan, but am concerned about how many tourists might be there. Does anyone know how often cruise ships dock at Roatan? Does the atmosphere of the island change with the sudden influx of people from cruise ships? Maybe I'd be better off going to Utila if I want to have a more relaxing time? Ah, so many questions!

Thanks for your responses.

1

Roatan is a much bigger island than Utila. Cruise ships tend to all have the same activities (cow-herding mentality). So there will be plenty of opportunities and places in Roatan to avoid the cruise ship crowds.

We were in Roatan for 10 days/9 nights in March (cruise ship season!) and I never noticed the cruise ship crowd.

Contact the Roatan tourism board and they might be able to send you a schedule. Or a local dive shop - sometimes they have the cruise ship schedules so they can adjust their dive boat captains/masters accordingly.

When we were on Roatan in March 2005, the cruise ship days were Tues, Wed, Thurs. Probably different now. We were there one of those days. You may want to avoid West End when the ships are in, as the shoppers do somewhat take over the town, which isn't that big to begin with. They didn't strike us as rude, though, just confused. They also had a kayak outing and some of them found their way to West Bay beach, but not in large numbers.

6

I had a completely different experience... West Bay Beach became unbearable when the ships were in (Tues - Thurs). Tuesday was the worst. It was also 2005 but I'm sure nothing has changed - if anything it has probably gotten worse as "adventure"/eco travel have become more popular.

100s of people were brought onto the beach - mostly to an area by the reef at West Bay where lounge chairs were set up for them. They took all the beach chairs at my hotel - Bananarama, and the owner Ron, didnt seem to care. Lots of vendors who were never there before showed up to sell things to the cruisers. Non stop soliciting...

I think if you avoid West End and West Bay, you're fine. Also, they also close down the canopy tour to cruise guests only when the ships are in.
