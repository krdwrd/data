Navy MWR Offers Assistance to Those Heading to Asia
Story Number: NNS060203-04
Release Date: 2/3/2006 12:48:00 PM

(Captain's Call Kit - Important Navy information prepared in an easy to distribute format.)

By Journalist 3rd Class Chris M. Hwang, Navy Personnel Command Communications

MILLINGTON, Tenn. (NNS) -- Navy Morale, Welfare and Recreation (MWR) can now provide Sailors a sneak peek of what foreign lands, such as Japan and other parts of Asia, have to offer through a new series of interactive CD-ROMs.

The CDs contain informative and entertaining videos about quality of life resources. The interactive function allows Sailors and their family members to link directly to Web sites that contain valuable information on travel, culture and other practical topics.

Topics include housing, currency rates and language. Links in the CD-ROM easily direct Sailors to apartment floor plans or convert dollars to yens.

To make shopping and traveling less challenging, the CD translates frequently used phrases from English to Japanese. Phrases range from “thank you” to “where’s the restaurant?”

“Instead of just getting a set of orders, Sailors can view entertaining videos that will give them an idea of what it would be like overseas,” said Ruel Odom, head of Marketing and Multimedia Development Branch for Community Support Programs. “On the CDs, Sailors, family members and civilian employees stationed in the selected overseas locations provide comments and recommendations regarding their experiences at duty stations in Asia.”

Odom, a retired master chief, has had a great impact on promoting exciting and rewarding billets across the world.

“The primary reason I decided to produce the CDs for overseas locations was due to my own experiences. My first overseas tour was in Japan. I was very apprehensive about going and had very little information about the country. I didn’t know what to expect. After I got there, I loved the experience, and spent over 12 years there, serving as command master chief for Naval Air Facility Misawa, U.S. Navy Forces Japan, and Carrier Air Wing 5,” said Odom.

Although the CDs are primarily focused on Japan, they also contain information about Diego Garcia, Guam, Korea and other countries in the region.

CD-ROMs for Europe and the Middle East are also in production. Sailors can request copies to learn more about Rota, Sigonella, Naples, Bahrain, Dubai and much more in Summer 2006.

To request a copy of Japan and Asia CD-ROM, visit www.mwr.navy.mil.

For related news, visit the Navy Personnel Command Navy NewsStand page at www.news.navy.mil/local/npc/.
